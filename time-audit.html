Automation Opportunities Draft
#1 — Highest Impact
Student support questions answer themselves.
Medium lift ~3h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
The same 5 questions — "Where is the recording?", "Can I switch cohorts?", "I missed a session", "What are the Zoom links?", "How do I access pre-work?" — are being answered manually 5–10 times per week across email and Slack. Each reply takes 2–5 minutes. The answers never change.
What the automated version looks like
A Claude-powered FAQ agent watches the AI Builder Slack workspace. When a student asks a known question, it replies instantly with the correct answer and links. For email, a Gmail filter + HubSpot workflow routes FAQ-subject emails to a pre-written response. Edge cases escalate to Slack DM. Kristen removed from first-line triage entirely.
#2 — High Impact
Onboarding emails stop confusing students before Session 1.
Quick win ~1.5h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
Students receive 5 overlapping automated emails at enrollment — flagged directly by a student. The confusion drives "what's my schedule?", "which email is correct?", and cohort-switch requests that land in Kristen's inbox. Fixing this is a root-cause fix for a large share of the reactive support load.
What the automated version looks like
Audit the HubSpot enrollment workflow with Macy/Ellie. Consolidate 5 emails into: (1) welcome + what to do now, (2) day-before reminder with Zoom link. Each email has exactly one job. Requires a 2h HubSpot session. Output: students arrive informed, cohort-switch requests drop significantly.
#3 — High Impact
Cohort switching happens without my involvement.
Medium lift ~1h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
Students who can't attend their enrolled cohort email Kristen to request a switch. She manually reviews, approves, and replies — sometimes 3–4 times to the same student. 5+ instances in the last 2 weeks. Fully delegatable or automatable.
What the automated version looks like
A self-serve form (Typeform or Leland-native) lets students select a new cohort date. Submission triggers a HubSpot workflow: updates enrollment, sends confirmation, notifies ops. Kristen gets a weekly Slack digest of all switches — not individual emails. Kristen removed from the loop entirely.
#4 — Medium Impact
Recording links reach students automatically within 30 minutes of each session.
Medium lift ~1h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
After each session, Zoom sends a recording-ready email. Someone (often Kristen) manually distributes the link via Slack or email. "Where is the recording?" is the #1 student support question — meaning distribution is delayed or inconsistent enough that students can't find it themselves.
What the automated version looks like
An n8n workflow watches for Zoom recording-ready emails (or Zoom webhook). When triggered, it extracts the recording URL and posts to the correct cohort Slack channel with a formatted message. Optionally sends a HubSpot email to enrolled students. Trigger: Zoom recording email. Output: recording in Slack within 30 min of session end. Zero manual steps.
#5 — Medium Impact
Weekly update to Nick writes itself in 2 minutes.
Quick win ~0.5h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
A weekly summary email to Nick is drafted manually by pulling from Notion, calendar, and memory. Each draft takes 20–40 minutes. The format is consistent week to week (this week / blockers / next week).
What the automated version looks like
A Claude Code skill (or n8n workflow) pulls this week's calendar events, Notion course status, and flagged blockers. Drafts a Nick-style weekly update in Kristen's voice. Review and send in under 2 minutes. Trigger: Thursday 3pm. Output: draft in correct voice, ready to paste into email.
#6 — Longer Term
LinkedIn presence runs on a publishing schedule without daily browsing.
Big build ~2h/week reclaimed
What's happening today
LinkedIn is the #1 most-visited domain (749 visits during work hours) — beating Gmail, Notion, and Claude combined. 68 visits/day on average. No publishing calendar visible in the data. Most of this is habitual checking, not intentional distribution work.
What the automated version looks like
A weekly 30-minute batch session with Claude generates 3–5 LinkedIn posts from actual AI Builder work: student wins, session highlights, lessons learned. Posts scheduled for the week via Buffer or native scheduling. LinkedIn becomes publish-and-exit instead of browse-constantly. Target: cut daily visit count from 68 to under 15.
