Sprint 1 — Time audit
What I learned about my time
Finding 01
I'm answering the same 5 questions manually — and it doesn't scale.
The same student questions (recordings, missed sessions, cohort switches) land in my inbox 5–10 times per week. One student got 4 separate replies to the same request. With 30 new students joining in a single week, this breaks at volume.
Finding 02
LinkedIn is my #1 browser domain — more visits than Gmail and Claude combined.
749 visits during work hours, ~68 per day. No publishing schedule in the data. That means most of it is reactive browsing, not intentional distribution — a significant drag on focused build time.
Finding 03
The onboarding email sequence is the root cause of my support load.
Students receive 5 overlapping automated emails at enrollment — flagged by a student directly. The confusion generates the schedule questions, cohort-switch requests, and "which email is right?" threads I field all week. Fixing it is a root-cause fix, not a patch.

Sprint 1 deliverable
What I'm building
#1  ·  Highest impact
Student support questions answer themselves.
~3h / week reclaimed Medium lift Not started
What's happening today
The same 5 questions — "Where is the recording?", "Can I switch cohorts?", "I missed a session", "What are the Zoom links?", "How do I access pre-work?" — are answered manually 5–10 times per week across email and Slack. The answers never change.
What the automated version looks like
A Claude-powered FAQ agent watches the AI Builder Slack workspace and replies instantly when a student asks a known question. Gmail filter + HubSpot workflow routes FAQ-subject emails to pre-written responses. Edge cases escalate via DM. Kristen removed from first-line triage entirely.
#2  ·  High impact
Onboarding emails stop confusing students before Session 1.
~1.5h / week reclaimed Quick win Not started
What's happening today
Students receive 5 overlapping automated emails at enrollment. The confusion drives schedule questions, cohort-switch requests, and "which email is right?" threads — all of which land in Kristen's inbox. A student flagged this directly.
What the automated version looks like
Audit the HubSpot enrollment workflow with Macy/Ellie. Consolidate 5 emails into 2: (1) welcome + what to do now, (2) day-before reminder with Zoom link. Each email has exactly one job. Students arrive informed. Cohort-switch requests drop significantly.
#3  ·  High impact
LinkedIn presence runs on a publishing schedule — not daily browsing.
~2h / week reclaimed Big build Not started
What's happening today
LinkedIn is the #1 most-visited domain at 749 work-hours visits — beating Gmail and Claude combined. ~68 visits per day with no publishing schedule visible in the data. Most of it is reactive browsing, not intentional distribution.
What the automated version looks like
A weekly 30-minute batch session with Claude generates 3–5 LinkedIn posts from actual AI Builder work: student wins, session highlights, lessons learned. Posts scheduled for the week. LinkedIn becomes publish-and-exit. Target: cut daily visits from 68 to under 15.
#4  ·  Medium impact
Cohort switching happens without my involvement.
~1h / week reclaimed Medium lift Not started
What's happening today
Students who can't attend their enrolled cohort email Kristen to request a switch. She manually reviews, approves, and replies — sometimes 3–4 times to the same student. 5+ instances in the last 2 weeks. Fully delegatable or automatable.
What the automated version looks like
A self-serve form lets students select a new cohort date. Submission triggers a HubSpot workflow: updates enrollment, sends a confirmation, notifies ops. Kristen gets a weekly Slack digest of all switches — not individual emails. Fully removed from the loop.
#5  ·  Medium impact
Recording links reach students automatically within 30 minutes of each session.
~1h / week reclaimed Medium lift Not started
What's happening today
After each session, Zoom sends a recording-ready email. Someone — often Kristen — manually distributes the link via Slack or email. "Where is the recording?" is the #1 student support question, meaning distribution is delayed or inconsistent.
What the automated version looks like
An n8n workflow watches for Zoom recording emails. When triggered, it extracts the recording URL and posts to the correct cohort Slack channel. Optionally sends a HubSpot email to enrolled students. Recording in Slack within 30 minutes of session end. Zero manual steps.
#6  ·  Lower impact
Weekly update to Nick writes itself in 2 minutes.
~0.5h / week reclaimed Quick win Not started
What's happening today
A weekly summary email to Nick is drafted manually by pulling from Notion, calendar, and memory. Each draft takes 20–40 minutes. The format is consistent week to week: this week / blockers / next week.
What the automated version looks like
A Claude Code skill pulls this week's calendar events, Notion course status, and flagged blockers. Drafts a Nick-style weekly update in Kristen's voice. Review and send in under 2 minutes. Trigger: Thursday 3pm.
